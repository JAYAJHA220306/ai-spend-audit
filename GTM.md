# Go To Market

## Who is the exact target user

Not "startups." The specific person is an **engineering manager or
technical co-founder at a 3-15 person startup** that has been running
for 6-18 months. They've recently started paying for multiple AI tools
as the team grew — one person got Cursor, someone else is on ChatGPT
Team, there's an Anthropic API bill showing up. Nobody has sat down
and audited whether all of it makes sense together.

Job title: Engineering Manager, CTO, or technical co-founder.
Company stage: post-product, pre-Series A. Probably 3-15 people.
Budget awareness: they see the bills but don't have a dedicated ops
person to optimize them yet.

## How to get the first 100 users in 30 days with $0

**Week 1 — Hacker News Show HN post**
"Show HN: I built a free tool to audit your AI tool spend"
HN is the single best channel for this. The audience is exactly
right — technical, cost-conscious, early adopters. A decent Show HN
post gets 200-500 visitors in 24 hours. Target: 30-40 signups.

**Week 2 — Reddit posts in relevant subreddits**
Post in r/SideProject, r/IndieHackers, r/ExperiencedDevs with a
genuine "I built this, here's what I found" framing. Not spam —
actually engage in the comments. Target: 20-30 signups per post.

**Week 3 — Twitter thread**
Write a thread: "I audited my AI tool spend and found I was wasting
$X/month. Here's what I found and the free tool I built to check
yours." Real numbers, real findings. Tag a few indie hacker accounts.
Target: 20-30 signups.

**Week 4 — Direct outreach**
DM 50 founders on Twitter who have tweeted about AI tool costs.
Personal message, not mass spam. "Hey, I built something that might
be relevant to your tweet about X." Target: 10-15 signups.
